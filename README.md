# Data_analysis_project_05
# S&P 500 Market Performance Analysis (2014–2017)

A comprehensive data analytics project isolating asset momentum, capital liquidity channels, seasonal market cycles, and risk-adjusted efficiency engines from a multi-year stock dataset containing 505 tickers and nearly 500,000 records.

## 🚀 Tech Stack & Workflow
* **Data Cleaning & Wrangling:** Python (`Pandas`) – Handled missing values, parsed chronological data structures, and precomputed cross-sectional baselines.
* **Quantitative Querying:** `SQL` – Built complex multi-stage CTEs and window functions (`LAG`, `ROW_NUMBER`) to model relative yields and day-over-day tracking metrics.
* **Exploratory Data Visualization:** Python (`Matplotlib` / `Seaborn`) – Generated distribution bar plots, risk-efficiency scatter frameworks, and sequential time-line matrices.

---

## 📊 Key Analytical Insights

### 1. Momentum & Compound Growth
* **Relative Leader:** **NVIDIA (NVDA)** generated an unparalleled **+1,120.05%** return, scaling from $15.86 to a terminal closing price of $193.50.
* **Nominal Cash Leader:** **Amazon (AMZN)** generated the largest raw price expansion, gaining **+$771.50** per share over the 4-year horizon.

### 2. Capital Liquidity Channels
* **Apple (AAPL)** heavily dominated institutional capital flow velocity, processing a staggering average of **$4.98 Billion** in transactional dollar volume daily.
* **Bank of America (BAC)** maintained the highest raw daily share turnover, averaging over **89.4 Million** traded shares.

### 3. Seasonality & Volatility Profiles
* **The February Effect:** When aggregated across all tickers over 4 years, **February** logged the strongest historical alpha baseline with an average daily market return of **+0.1888%**.
* **Risk Profiles:** **Chesapeake Energy (CHK)** registered as the most volatile node with a daily standard deviation of **4.55%**, while **DXC Technology (DXC)** and **Hilton (HLT)** demonstrated the most optimal risk-adjusted efficiency ratios.
